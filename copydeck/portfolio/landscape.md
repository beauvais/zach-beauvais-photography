## SECTION: INTRO TEXT

**H2:** `Photography as attention`

```
Before I worked with people, I worked with everything else — the surfaces, textures, and
light of the natural and built world. Landscape and abstract photography is, at its core,
an exercise in attention: learning to notice what's already there, rather than constructing
something new.

That practice shapes how I approach portraiture. The same patience, the same attentiveness
to atmosphere, the same willingness to wait — it all carries over. These images are both
a practice in themselves and the foundation of everything else I do.
```

> **Notes:** "Photography as attention" is a strong H2 — it's compact and unusual enough to stick. "Learning to notice what's already there, rather than constructing something new" is the philosophical heart of this section and connects directly back to the portrait approach described on `how-i-work.astro`. "A practice in themselves and the foundation of everything else I do" — this sentence does double work, validating the landscape work on its own terms while also connecting it to the portrait practice. Keep both sentences; don't merge them.
