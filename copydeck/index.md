<!-- draft-v2 (cornfield removed):
My sessions suit people who want something personal and considered —

-->

```
There's a whole genre of portrait photography in the midwest —
golden hour, open fields, that particular quality of late-afternoon
light. It's beautiful work, and plenty of talented photographers do
it well. It's just not what I do.

My sessions suit people who want something more personal and considered —
for a professional brand, a meaningful milestone, or simply because
you've never had a portrait you actually liked. If you have something
you want to express but aren't sure how to say it yet, that's exactly
where we start.
```

*The first paragraph does the positioning work without the sneer: it names the genre, calls it beautiful, credits the people who do it, and steps out of it gracefully. "It's just not what I do" is as direct as it needs to be. No Hoosier feels insulted; you've just made clear where you sit. The second paragraph is the invitation.*
